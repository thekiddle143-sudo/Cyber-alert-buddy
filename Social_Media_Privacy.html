Social Media Privacy: Guarding Your Digital Life

Understand how your data is used and master the settings needed to protect your personal information on all social platforms. **Duration: 10 minutes.**

Your social profile is a valuable target for cybercriminals.

The Privacy Problem: Why Sharing is Riskier Now (2 min)

Every piece of information you post, from your pet's name to your vacation photos, can be used by criminals in two primary ways:

  • **Targeted Phishing:** Criminals use your public posts to craft highly believable, personalized scams (Spear Phishing). If they know you just bought a new car, they can send a convincing "insurance" email.
  • **Security Questions:** Many passwords and bank accounts still use security questions like "What was your first car?" or "What city did you meet your spouse?" If that information is public on your profile, you've essentially given away your security answers.

Module 1: Your Social Media Privacy Audit (4 min)

Perform this audit on your top three social media accounts (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, etc.):

  • Lock It Down: Set your profile to **"Friends Only"** or **"Private."** Even on platforms like LinkedIn, restrict who can see your contact info.
  • Review Geo-tagging: Turn off **Location Services** for social media apps. Revealing your exact location tells criminals when you're away from home.
  • Friends List Visibility: On platforms like Facebook, hide your friends list. Hackers often clone profiles, and a visible friends list makes their job easier.
  • Third-Party Apps: Go to your account settings and **revoke access** for any old quizzes, games, or apps you haven't used in six months. These apps often retain access to your personal data.

The Vacation Rule:

**Never post pictures of your vacation until you are safely back home.** Posting real-time travel updates is essentially an open invitation to burglars.

Module 2: Limiting Data Sharing (4 min)

Social media companies track your activity both on and off their platforms to serve ads. You can reduce this data collection:

  • **Off-Facebook Activity:** Many platforms have a setting that tracks the websites you visit *after* leaving their app. **Find this setting and clear/turn off this tracking.**
  • **Ad Preferences:** Disable "Ad Personalization" or "Ad Tracking" in your settings. While you will still see ads, they won't be based on the deepest data mining of your personal life.
  • **Profile Consolidation:** Consider whether you truly need accounts on five different platforms. Deleting unused accounts eliminates potential security risks and data exposure.

Action Item: Go to your security settings now and ensure Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) is enabled on all your active social media accounts.
